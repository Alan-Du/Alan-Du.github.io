Hull White 1F model (Const. Version) calibration in PYTHON¶

--- Alan Du, 07-21-2020, Shaolun.du@gmail.com

In this notebook, I will calibrate HW1F(const. $a$ and $\sigma$) model from scratch in python based on Libor curve and swaption volatility surface.

As in my previous post, HW1F model has the SDE dynamics as following, for simplicity, we will assume single curve framework (Discounting curve and Forward curve will be the same, in real word, they will be two different curves normally LIBOR for forawrd curve and OIS for discounting and that is called dual curve stripping):

HullWhite model (Const. Version) \begin{equation} dr(t) = (\theta(t) - a*r(t)) dt + \sigma dW_t \end{equation}

  • $\theta(t)$ is time dependent long term mean rate.

  • $a$ is const. mean reversion speed.

  • $\sigma$ is const. volatility.

  • $r(0) = r_{0}$ the current yield curve instantaneous forward rate.

We directly have some basic information about this SDE:

$$ \begin{aligned} E[r(t)|F_s] &= \frac{E(s)}{E(t)}+a-\frac{E(s)}{E(t)}*a\\ Var[r(t)|F_s] &= V_r(s,t) \end{aligned} $$

To calibrate this model we must solve for $\theta(t) , a , \sigma$.

Here, $\theta(t)$ can be calibrated from initial yield curve. $\sigma$ and $\alpha$ we assume it is const. and not change with time.

We will do the following steps:

  • Prerequisit: Derive swaption pricing formula under HW1F short rate model dynamic.
  • Build initial yield curve for USD LIBOR.
  • Get a market panel information of swaptions volatilities and convert them into prices.
  • Calculate instantaneous forward rate $f(0,t)$ and all discount factors $P(0,t)$ based on initial yield curve.
  • Solve an optimization problem as $$Min(\sum_{i}{(\frac{swo^{market}_{i}-swo^{model}_{i}}{swo^{market}_{i}})^2})$$ with $\sigma$ and $a$ as variables.

Here, $f(0,t)$ means the instantaneous forward rate fixing at time 0 and mature at time t. Mathematically,

$$f(0,t) = -\frac{\partial ln(P(0,t))}{\partial t}$$

From the SDE we can solve for model discount factors: $$ \begin{aligned} P(t,T) &= A(t,T)*e^{-B(t,T)*r_0}\\ r_0 &= inst. fwd\ rate\ at\ 0 \end{aligned} $$ Also, we have defination for $A(t,T), B(t,T)$ and $E(t)$:

$$ \begin{aligned} E(t) &= e^{\int_{0}^{t}a(u)du} = e^{a*t}\\ B(t,T) &= E(t)\int_{t}^{T}\frac{du}{E(u)} = \frac{1}{a}*(1-e^{a*(T-t)})\\ A(t,T) &= ln\frac{P(0,T)}{P(0,t)} + B(t,T)f(0,t)-\frac{1}{2}B(t,T)^2*V_{r}(0,t)\\ V_{r}(s,t) &= \frac{1}{E^2(t)}*\int_{s}^{t}E^2(u)\sigma^2(u)du \end{aligned} $$

Step One: $\sigma$ and $a$ should be calibrated into swaption volatility surface¶

Let's assume we have a receiver swaption with maturity $T_{0}$, tenor $T_{n}$ and strike $K$. So the underlying swap is a receiv fixed payment swap starting from $T_{0}$ and maturied at $T_{n}$, it has two legs which exchange payment at different times normally, the floating leg pays quaterly and the fixed leg pays semiannually. The ATM stike $K_{ATM}$ for this swap can be decided as:

$$ K_{ATM} = \frac{\sum_{i}{Fwd Rate(0,T_{i-1}^{f},T_{i}^{f})*P(0,T_{i}^{f})}}{\sum_{i}{Fixed Rate(0,T_{i-1}^{c},T_{i}^{c})*P(0,T_{i}^{c})}} $$

In this case, the receiver swaption value at time $t$ with strike $K$ can be written as:

$$ swo_{pay}(t,T,K) = E[B(t)^{-1}*(\sum{S_{i}P(t,T_{i}^{f})}-\sum{C_{j}P(t,T_{j}^{c})})] $$

Here, we exclude the final payment of this swap since they will certainly offset each other. The $C_{j}$ terms are fixed rate coupon rates, $S_{i}$ terms are floating rate reset rates. All $P(t,T)$ are discounting factor at time $t$ from time $T$. For example, US swap market standard convention says the fixed rate coupon paying is 6 month and the floating rate paying is 3 month. So $C_{j}$ terms will be 6 month seperate from each other and $S_{i}$ terms will be 3 months seperate from each other.

In order to solve this expectation calculation, we have to use Jamshidian trick and finally arrive the following(I am currently not able to derive SDE calculation that by hand...):

$$ \begin{aligned} swo_{pay}(K,T_{0},T_{p}) &= \sum_{1<=i<=n}{c_{i}ZBP(T_{0},T_{i},X_{i})} \\ c_{i} &= K\delta(T_{i-1},T_{i})\\ c_{n} &= 1+K\delta(T_{n-1},T_{n})\\ X_{i} &= e^{A(T_0,T_i)-B(T_0,T_i)r^*} \end{aligned} $$

where $r^{*}$ is solved from equation below: $$ \sum_{i}{c_{i}*e^{A(T_{0},T_{i})-B(T_{0},T_{i})r^*}} =1 $$

Here, $P(t,T,r)$ means discount factor at time $t$, end at time $T$ with annualized rate $r$. So this equition tells us the fixed coupon receiving cash flow should equal to floating leg paying cash flow under rates $r^*$.

And $ZBP(T_F,T_P,X)$ is zero bond call option price:

$$ \begin{aligned} ZBP(T_F,T_P,X) &= X*P(0,T_F)*\phi(d_{+}) - P(0,T_P)*\phi(d_{-}) \\ d_{+(-)} &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{V_P(0,T_{F},T_{P})}}*log(\frac{P(0,T_F)*X}{P(0,T_P)})+(-)\frac{\sqrt{V_P(0,T_{F},T_{P})}}{2}\\ \end{aligned} $$

where $V_P(0,T_{F},T_{P})$ stands for volatility accumulation given as below:

$$ \begin{aligned} V_P(t,T_{F},T_{P}) &= \int_{t}^{T_F} \sigma^2(B(u,T_P)-B(u,T_F))^2du = V_r(t,T_F)B(T_F,T_P)^2\\ V_{r}(s,t) &= \frac{1}{E^2(t)}*\int_{s}^{t}E^2(u)\sigma^2du \end{aligned} $$

A quick takeway here shows that the pricing of swaption under hull-white model basically turns into a sun of bunch call options on zero coupon bonds.

Tools functions below:¶

  • ZBP pricing: $ZBP_{price}(T_F,T_P,X)$, this function returns pricing of zero bond call option given a.
  • Solver for $r^*$: $solver_r(c,s,dfs)$ this funtion solve for the unique $r^*$ satistify $ \sum_{i}{c_{i}e^{A(T_{0},T_{i})-B(T_{0},T_{i})r^*}} =1$

# Plot and check bond price under Hull White model
def _E(t):
    # Calculate E
    ans = math.exp(thismodule._a*t)
    return ans

def _reciprocal_E(u):
    # Calculate reciprocal of E
    ans = _E(u)
    return 1/ans

def _E2_Sigma2(t):
    # Calculate piecewise integration of E
    sig_val = thismodule._sigma
    E = _E(t)
    ans = sig_val*sig_val*E*E
    return ans

def _Vr(s,t):
    # Inputs a and sigma are time dependent lists starting with [0,0]
    res, err = integrate.quad(_E2_Sigma2, s, t)
    e = _E(t)
    return res/(e*e)

def _B(t,T):
    # Calculate integral funtion B
    res, err = integrate.quad(_reciprocal_E, t, T)
    return _E(t)*res

def _A(t,T,Bt_T,Vr0_t):
    # Calculate function A
    sig_val = thismodule._sigma
    a_t = thismodule._a
    P0_T = _cs_dfs(T)
    P0_t = _cs_dfs(t)
    f0_t = _cs_inst_fwd(t)
    a = P0_T/P0_t*math.exp(Bt_T*f0_t-1/2*Bt_T*Bt_T*Vr0_t)
    return a

def _model_P0_Tf(T_x):
    # return model price of Zero coupon bond
    # given information on t=0
    r0 = thismodule._r0
    Vr0_t = thismodule._Vr_dict[(0,T_x)]
    B0_T = thismodule._B_dict[(0,T_x)]
    A0_T = thismodule._A_dict[(0,T_x)]
    bond_price = A0_T*math.exp(-B0_T*r0)
    return bond_price

def _V_P0(T_F,T_P):
    # Simplied version of V_P
    # Calculate accumulated volatility
    B = thismodule._B_dict[(T_F,T_P)]
    Vr = thismodule._Vr_dict[(0,T_F)]
    return B*B*Vr

def _ZBP(T_F,T_P,X):
    # Zero coupon call option pricing under hull-white 1F model
    P0_Tf = _model_P0_Tf(T_F)
    P0_Tp = _model_P0_Tf(T_P)
    V0_Tf_Tp = _V_P0(T_F,T_P)
    d1 = 1/math.sqrt(V0_Tf_Tp)*math.log(P0_Tf*X/P0_Tp) + math.sqrt(V0_Tf_Tp)/2
    d2 = d1-math.sqrt(V0_Tf_Tp)
    price = X*P0_Tf*norm.cdf(d1) - P0_Tp*norm.cdf(d2)
    return price

def _Pswo(K, r_star, pay_dates):
    # Calculate payer swaption price
    sum_f = 0
    T_0 = pay_dates[0]
    Vr_0_t = thismodule._Vr_dict[(0,T_0)]
    for loc in range(1,len(pay_dates)):
        ele = pay_dates[loc]
        c_i = (ele-pay_dates[loc-1])/_year_days*K
        if loc == len(pay_dates)-1:
            c_i += 1
        B = thismodule._B_dict[(T_0,ele)]
        A = thismodule._A_dict[(T_0,ele)]
        X_i = A*math.exp(-B*r_star)
        ZBP_price = _ZBP(T_0,ele,X_i)
        sum_f += c_i*ZBP_price
    return sum_f

def _gen_swap_tb( start, maturity, pay_freq):
    # Swaption table generate
    def diff_month(d1, d2):
        return (d2.year - d1.year) * 12 + d2.month - d1.month
    # Generate swap payment table
    pay_dates = [start+relativedelta(months = pay_freq*i) for i in range(int(diff_month(start, maturity)/pay_freq)+1)]
    pay_dates = [(ele-_sdate).days for ele in pay_dates]
    # Return pay_date are in years unit
    return pay_dates

def _solve_ATM_strike(pay_dates):
    sum_f = 0
    T_0 = pay_dates[0]
    Model_P0 = _model_P0_Tf(T_0)
    sum_dis = 0
    for loc in range(1,len(pay_dates)):
        T_pre = pay_dates[loc-1]
        T_i = pay_dates[loc]
        Model_Pi = _model_P0_Tf(T_i)
        sum_dis += Model_Pi*(T_i-T_pre)/_year_days
        if loc == len(pay_dates)-1:
            Model_Pn = Model_Pi
    K = (Model_P0-Model_Pn)/sum_dis
    return K

def _solve_r(r_star, K, pay_dates):
    # Solver for r* 
    T_0 = pay_dates[0]
    sum_f = 0
    Vr_0_t = thismodule._Vr_dict[(0,T_0)]
    for loc in range(1,len(pay_dates)):
        ele = pay_dates[loc]
        c_i = (ele-pay_dates[loc-1])/_year_days*K
        if loc == len(pay_dates)-1:
            c_i += 1
        B = thismodule._B_dict[(T_0,ele)]
        A = thismodule._A_dict[(T_0,ele)]
        sum_f += c_i*A*math.exp(-B*r_star)
    return sum_f-1

def _update_model_state(swaption_li, pay_freq):
    # Clean all states variables
    thismodule._A_dict = {}
    thismodule._B_dict = {}
    thismodule._Vr_dict = {}
    thismodule._Vp_dict = {}
    for swap in swaption_li:
        swap_start = _sdate+relativedelta(years = swap[0])
        swap_end = swap_start+relativedelta(years = swap[1])
        pay_dates = _gen_swap_tb(swap_start,swap_end,pay_freq)
        T0 = pay_dates[0]
        for pdt in pay_dates:
            # Cached all states variables
            Vr_0_t = _Vr(0,pdt)
            if (0,pdt) not in thismodule._A_dict:
                B = _B(0,pdt)
                A = _A(0,pdt,B,Vr_0_t)
                thismodule._A_dict[(0,pdt)] = A
                thismodule._B_dict[(0,pdt)] = B
                thismodule._Vr_dict[(0,pdt)] = Vr_0_t
            if (T0,pdt) not in thismodule._A_dict:
                B_T0 = _B(T0,pdt)
                A_T0 = _A(T0,pdt,B_T0,Vr_0_t)
                thismodule._A_dict[(T0,pdt)] = A_T0
                thismodule._B_dict[(T0,pdt)] = B_T0
                V0_Tf_Tp = _V_P0(T0,pdt)
                thismodule._Vp_dict[(T0,pdt)] = V0_Tf_Tp

def _multi_var_wrap(x0):
    opt_residual = 0
    # Update global variable before optimization
    thismodule._a = x0[0]
    thismodule._sigma = x0[1]
    pay_freq = 6
    _update_model_state(_market_swaption, pay_freq )

        K = _solve_ATM_strike(pay_dates)
        r_star = fsolve(_solve_r, 0, args=(K,pay_dates))
        P_model = _Pswo(K,r_star,pay_dates)
        P_diff = (ele[2]-P_model)/ele[2]
        opt_residual += P_diff*P_diff
    return opt_residual

Part Two: Theta(t) should be calibrated into initial yield curve term structure¶

For the function $\theta(t)$ involved in the short rate dynamic and ensuring exact replication of the initial curve with $a$ and $\sigma$ given, we have:

$$ \begin{aligned} \theta(t) &= \frac{\partial{f(0,t)}}{\partial t} + a* f(0,t)+\frac{1}{2}(\frac{\partial^2 V(0,t)}{\partial t^2}+a*\frac{\partial V(0,t)}{\partial t}) \\ &= \frac{\partial{f(0,t)}}{\partial t} + a* f(0,t)+\frac{\sigma^2}{2a}(1-e^{-at}) \\ V(t,T) &= \int_{t}^{T}\sigma^2 du = (T-t)*\sigma^2\\ \sigma(u,T) &= \sigma(u)B(u,T) \end{aligned} $$

Here $f(t,T)$ is the initial instantaneour forward rate fixing at t mature at T.

In this notebook, we will assume the yield curve is constructed already and we get a list of discount factors with numerical dates as index.

Details on curve bootstrapping please refer to my previous post on yield curve construction.

Part Three: Simulation future interests rate path based on optimization output¶

Going back to our original SDE: \begin{equation} dr(t) = (\theta(t) - a*r(t)) dt + \sigma dW_t \end{equation}

We have solved, $\theta$, $a$ and $\sigma$. Next step is to simulate future interests path using Monte Carlo simulation method. Starting point is $r_0$ which is the instantaneous forward rate at current level. Then, we will start generate random normal variables $N(0,1)$ as $dW_t$. Plug into SDE equation to arrive next instantaneous forward rate $r_t$. Eventually, we link all random instantaneous rates together to have a spot interests rate curve and so does the discount factors.

N_path = 1000
delta_t = 30 # Timestep is one month
_time_grid = list(range(0,_init_dfs[-1][0],delta_t))

    t_time = _time_grid[t_loc]
    theta = _Theta(t_time)
    s_n = np.random.normal(0, 1, N_path).T
    pre_r = short_rate_paths[:,t_loc-1]
    drt = (theta-thismodule._a*pre_r)*delta_t+thismodule._sigma*math.sqrt(delta_t)*s_n
    short_rate_paths[:,t_loc] = pre_r+drt
    spot_rate_paths[:,t_loc] = np.sum(short_rate_paths[:,:t_loc],axis=1)*delta_t/t_time
